Branding & Color Strategy — 5 Sites

Each site runs a Committed color strategy (one saturated color carries the surface). The first-order reflex — contractor = navy blue — was rejected for all 5. The second-order reflex — "not blue, so orange" — was also rejected. Each palette was derived from the physical scene of a stressed homeowner searching in the Central Valley heat.

Site 1 · Merced
🏠 Roofing
Strategy: Committed · Terracotta/Clay
Base
Primary
Surface
CTA
Scene: 95°F in Merced. Homeowner sees water stain on ceiling — searches on phone in mild panic. Needs someone who shows up today.
Rejected: Navy + grey (reflex #1). Black + orange (reflex #2).
Reference: Old California mission tile supplier's catalog — warm, confident, earthen. Clay tile roofing IS the physical material of this region.
Font direction: A compact, confident sans with strong weight contrast. Avoid Inter/Plus Jakarta (banned). Look at Barlow Condensed or Rubik.

CTA
Scene: 105°F in Hanford. AC died at noon. Searching on phone, sweating. "HVAC near me NOW." Needs technical confidence, not friendliness.
Rejected: Sky blue / glacier blue (reflex #1 — obvious "coolness"). Blue gradient on white (reflex #2).
Reference: A refrigerant pressure gauge. An industrial instrument panel. Dark, precise, technical — not a spa ad.
Font direction: A tight, technical sans with strong numerals. Look at Geist, Azeret Mono for labels, or Manrope.

CTA
Scene: Pipe burst under the kitchen sink in Madera. 7am. Water on the floor. Need someone in the next hour — not next week.
Rejected: Navy blue (reflex #1 — every plumber). Cobalt blue (reflex #2 — still blue).
Reference: Copper pipe fittings and bronze valves — the literal material of plumbing. A 1970s plumbing supply catalog: honest, no-nonsense, warm.
Font direction: A direct, no-frills workhorse sans. Look at Figtree or Nunito Sans (not banned — strong numerals, warm feel).

Base
Sage

CTA
Scene: Homeowner in Tulare, backyard, looking at a large branch down after a windstorm. Worried about liability. Not panicked — needs calm expertise.
Rejected: Forest green + brown (reflex #1 — every tree company). Dark green + yellow (reflex #2 — John Deere territory).
Reference: California State Parks wayfinding system — outdoorsy but institutional. Drought-resistant landscape sage, not golf-course green.
Font direction: A confident humanist sans. Look at Raleway or Josefin Sans for headings (check if on reflex list — neither is banned).

Scene: New homeowner in Madera, surveying the chain-link fence left by the previous owner. Dog needs containment. Calm decision, not emergency.
Rejected: Wood brown + texture (reflex #1 — every fence site). Grey + navy (reflex #2).
Reference: High-end residential architecture hardware store brand. A chalk line snapped on pavement. Precision, grounded, zero decoration.
Font direction: A geometric sans with strong structure. Look at Urbanist or Epilogue (not on banned list, architectural feel).
Note: All colors use OKLCH — they stay perceptually uniform across screens. No pure black (#000) or white (#fff) used anywhere. Each neutral is tinted toward its brand hue (chroma 0.015–0.02). CTAs are always the highest-chroma swatch per site.
