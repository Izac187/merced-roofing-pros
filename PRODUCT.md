# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Merced County homeowners (ages 35–65, middle-income) who need roofing work. They land via Google after a storm, a visible leak, or a scheduled inspection. They're searching on mobile, often stressed about damage, unfamiliar with roofing costs, and worried about being ripped off. Their job: find a trustworthy local roofer fast and get a realistic price before committing. They compare 2–3 options before calling.

## Product Purpose

Rank-and-rent lead generation site. The site owner captures homeowner inquiries (form fill or phone call) and sells or passes leads to a local roofing contractor in Merced, CA. The contractor closes the job. Success = a completed form submission or inbound call. The contractor relationship may change, so the site must never be locked to a single contractor's pricing or branding.

## Brand Personality

Direct, Local, Trustworthy. Central Valley plain-spoken — not a franchise, not a luxury brand, not a bargain basement. The site is the homeowner's advocate: fast information, no runaround, fair deal.

## Anti-references

- HomeAdvisor / Angi / Thumbtack — aggregator feel, not local
- Generic SaaS-clean (white + blue, corporate, stock photos of smiling teams)
- National franchise roofing (e.g., GAF Master Elite dealer branding — too polished, makes the homeowner feel like a number)
- Sketchy contractor sites (Comic Sans, clipart, dark backgrounds with neon text)
- Luxury home improvement brands — wrong price signal for a value-focused position

## Design Principles

1. **Value is earned, not stated.** Don't say "best value in Merced." Show it: fast callback, free estimate, no hidden steps. Let the structure make the argument.
2. **Speed is a trust signal.** 30-minute callback is the headline promise. Every element of the UI should reinforce that this is fast and local, not corporate and slow.
3. **Remove every exit.** Each section should funnel toward the form or the phone number. No content exists for its own sake; it exists to convert.
4. **Local over generic.** Merced County specificity beats professional photography. Name neighborhoods, quote local rates, reference the Central Valley climate.
5. **Contractor-agnostic.** No contractor name, no crew photos, no pricing commitments. The site is a surface, not a business card.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Large touch targets for mobile (44px minimum). High contrast text on dark backgrounds. No critical information conveyed by color alone. Readable at default browser font sizes.
